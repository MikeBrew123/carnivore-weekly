7-Day Carnivore Starter Plan | Day 3 of 7

Your no-nonsense shopping list.

Hey,

One of the best things about carnivore is how short your grocery list gets. No more wandering through 15 aisles trying to figure out which grain-free, sugar-free, organic, non-GMO snack bar is "healthy." You're going to the meat counter. That's basically it.

Here's what to grab for your first week. I've broken it into tiers so you can match it to your budget.

The Essentials (start here)

  • Ground beef, 80/20 (3-5 lbs). The fat matters. Don't buy lean.
  • Eggs (2 dozen). Cheap, versatile, packed with nutrients.
  • Butter (1 lb). Real butter. Not margarine.
  • Salt. Sea salt or pink Himalayan. Use it generously.

Add if your budget allows

  • Ribeye or NY strip steaks (2-4). You earned something nice this week.
  • Bacon (1 lb). No sugar-added if you can find it.
  • Beef liver (1/2 lb). Not required, but it's the most nutrient-dense food on earth.
  • Heavy cream or cheese. Some people do great with dairy. Test it later.

Nice to have

  • Bone broth (or bones to make your own). Great for electrolytes.
  • Beef tallow or ghee for cooking.
  • Sardines or salmon (canned is fine). Omega-3s.

Meal prep tip:

Cook 2-3 pounds of ground beef on Sunday. Season it with salt. Store it in the fridge. That's lunch for the week. When you're hungry, reheat a bowl and add butter. Done in 90 seconds. No recipe required.

A few things to skip for now: pork rinds (they'll make you overeat), jerky (usually has sugar), and anything that says "carnivore-friendly" on a fancy label. You don't need special products. You need meat, salt, water.

If money is tight, ground beef and eggs will carry you. I've seen people thrive on nothing but those two things and salt. Don't let budget be the reason you don't try this. We actually wrote a full $7/day survival guide if you want to see how far a tight budget can go.

Tomorrow I'm going to tell you about the number one mistake new carnivores make. Spoiler: almost everyone does it, and it's the reason most people feel lousy their first week.
