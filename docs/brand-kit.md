## **UNIVERSAL WRITING STANDARDS**

**ALL agents follow these rules:**

### Reading Level
- **Target**: Grade 8-10 (Flesch-Kincaid 60-70)
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Define jargon when introduced.

### Authenticity
- **Contractions required**: Use "don't," "can't," "it's," "we're" naturally
- **No em-dashes**: Use periods, commas, or colons instead
- **Active voice preferred**: "Sarah found" not "It was found by Sarah"
- **Direct address**: Speak to "you" when relevant

### AI Tell Words (BANNED)
- ❌ "Delve" / "Delving"
- ❌ "Robust"
- ❌ "Leverage" (as verb)
- ❌ "Navigate" (metaphorically)
- ❌ "Crucial" / "Critical" (overuse)
- ❌ "Realm" / "Landscape" / "Tapestry"
- ❌ "Utilize" (just say "use")
- ❌ "Facilitate"
- ❌ "It's important to note that..."
- ❌ "Furthermore" / "Moreover" (use "Also" or period + new sentence)

### Structure Rules
- **Opening**: Hook first (question, surprising fact, or relatable observation)
- **Spacing**: White space is your friend. Short paragraphs.
- **Lists**: Use bullets or numbers for clarity
- **Emphasis**: Bold for key points, not ALL CAPS
- **Examples**: Specific > generic. Real > hypothetical.

### Evidence & Claims
- **Any health claim**: Cite source or say "based on what I've seen"
- **Specific numbers**: Include metrics (105 mg/dL, not "high glucose")
- **Edge cases**: Acknowledge: "Your situation might be different"

### Length
- **Blog posts**: 800-1200 words
- **Short posts**: 300-500 words
- **Headlines**: Active, specific ("How to Break Weight Loss Stalls" not "Breaking Stalls")
