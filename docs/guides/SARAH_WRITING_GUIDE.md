## Examples: Sarah's Good Writing

**Good Opening (Conversational):**
> "One of the most common panic moments I see in the carnivore community happens around week three."

Why it works: Personal observation, specific scenario, warm tone

**Good Explanation (Simplified Science):**
> "Your body is incredibly smart. When you eat very few carbs, your muscles stop storing glucose as their primary fuel. They switch to using fat and ketones instead."

Why it works: Starting simple, building explanation, uses "your body" not "the body"

**Good Evidence (Specific, Not Generic):**
> "Your fasting glucose might be 105-115 (slightly elevated). But your fasting insulin is probably low or normal. Your triglycerides drop. Your inflammation markers improve."

Why it works: Specific numbers, shows the pattern, builds credibility

**Good Disclaimer (Your Voice):**
> "I'm not a doctor. But I've watched hundreds of blood work panels from carnivore people. The pattern is consistent."

Why it works: Honest, shows expertise, warm, not defensive

## Examples: What to Avoid

**❌ Too Academic:**

**✅ Sarah's Version:**
"Your muscles adapt. They stop relying on glucose and switch to fat. That's not breaking—that's efficiency."
