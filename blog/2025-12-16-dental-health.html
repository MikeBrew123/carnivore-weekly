Carnivore and Dental Health: What Actually Happens

Does eating only meat damage your teeth? Common concern. Let me address it directly.

The Concern

Conventional wisdom: low-carb diets cause tooth decay because bacteria need carbs. But no carbs = no fuel for bacteria = teeth should be fine, right?

Reality is slightly more complex.

What Actually Happens

Good news: Carnivore doesn't inherently damage teeth. No sugar = no fuel for cavity-causing bacteria. Your mouth pH stays stable (no acid spikes from carbs).

Potential issue: High-fat diet can alter oral microbiome. Some people report different plaque formation. Not necessarily bad, just different.

What the Data Shows

No strong evidence carnivore damages teeth. Anecdotally: most people report stable or improved dental health. Some report different plaque texture (normal).

How to Maintain Dental Health

  • Brush twice daily (obviously)
  • Floss (especially with meat diet—fibers can get stuck)
  • Water rinse after meals
  • See dentist annually (get professional cleaning)
  • Don't grind teeth (stress management)

Bottom Line

Carnivore is actually good for teeth because no sugar. Just maintain normal hygiene. Your teeth will be fine.
