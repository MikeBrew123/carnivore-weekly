5 Most-Asked Beginner Questions (And You're Not Alone)

I monitor the carnivore community across Reddit, YouTube, TikTok, Discord, and Twitter. And right now? New Year's is bringing a flood of people with the same questions. These aren't obscure issues‚Äîthey're the things everyone worries about but feels awkward asking.

Here's what I'm seeing asked over and over. You're definitely not alone in wondering these things.

Question #1: "How Much Does This Actually Cost?"

What people are asking: "I want to try this but beef is expensive. Can I do carnivore on a budget?"

The real answer: Yes, if you're smart about it. The community conversation has shifted a lot. Nobody's pretending ribeyes are cheap. But ground beef, chuck roasts, organ meats, and eggs are affordable. You can do this for $3-5 per day if you're in the US.

Check our budget guide in the wiki‚Äîit has the actual numbers and strategies the community uses. And yes, buying in bulk from warehouse stores helps a lot.

Question #2: "Wait, I Don't Need Carbs?"

What people are asking: "Everyone says you need carbs. Isn't this crazy?"

The real answer: You don't need dietary carbs. Your body can run on fat and protein. But this is the part that freaks people out, so let me be clear: this is not mainstream medical advice. This is what works for a lot of people, but it's not what most doctors recommend. If you're skeptical, that's not wrong. Talk to your doctor about whether this makes sense for you specifically.

The research exists and it's solid, but the cultural programming runs deep. Your skepticism is healthy.

Question #3: "What About My Family/Social Life?"

What people are asking: "How do I explain this to my mom? How do I eat at restaurants? What do I do at a dinner party?"

The real answer: This is the hardest part, and the community feels this one hard. Here's what works: Order meat and vegetables, eat the meat. Don't make a speech about it. People get less weird when you don't make it weird. Your family will probably make comments. That's on them, not you.

At a restaurant, it's easy‚Äîjust order a steak or burger without the bun. Most places are cool with it. At a family dinner, eat the meat on your plate and skip the rest. You don't need to convince anyone. Just let your results speak.

Question #4: "Is This Dangerous?"

What people are asking: "People on the internet say this is dangerous. Am I hurting myself?"

The real answer: There's legitimate debate about this. Some people see dramatic health improvements. Others have concerns. If you have a doctor you trust, involve them. If you have existing health conditions, you absolutely need medical supervision. Don't make this change alone if you're on medications or dealing with anything serious.

This is worth taking seriously. Your health matters more than proving a point.

Question #5: "Why Am I So Tired/Weird Right Now?"

What people are asking: "I've been doing this for a week and I feel terrible. Did I mess up?"

The real answer: You're probably in adaptation. Your body is shifting how it produces energy. This usually feels weird for 2-4 weeks. It's not fun, but it's normal. Sarah covers this deeply in her beginner blueprint post.

If you're still feeling terrible after 4 weeks, it's almost always salt or fat intake. Not the diet itself.

What the Community Gets Right

The carnivore community is honestly pretty supportive right now. People ask these questions and other people answer with real experience, not dogma. You don't get a lot of "just trust me, bro." You get "I tried this, it didn't work, here's what did work."

That's valuable. Community experience is data.

What the Community Gets Wrong

The flip side: there's a lot of overcomplicated advice. People recommend things that sound fancy but don't matter. There's also a cultural tendency to oversell the diet. Not everyone who tries carnivore gets magical results. Some people try it, feel okay, and go back to normal eating. That's fine too.

The honest truth: carnivore is simple in theory. The actual obstacles are adaptation, social pressure, and cost. Not magic. Not science-defying. Just a dietary approach that works for some people better than others.

The Question Nobody Asks But Should

"What if this doesn't work for me?" And the answer is: that's okay. Not everyone thrives on carnivore. Some people try it, adapt fine, feel slightly better, and decide it's not worth the social friction. That's a legitimate outcome. You don't need to make yourself miserable to prove something.

Starting Right

If you're thinking about starting, here's what I'd do based on what I see working:

  1. Educate yourself first. Read Sarah's beginner guide. Understand what adaptation looks like.
  2. Talk to your doctor if you have any health conditions. Don't skip this.
  3. Plan for weeks 2-3 to be weird. Buy salt. Buy fatty meat. Know what's coming.
  4. Don't tell everyone immediately. Keep it quiet until you see how you feel.
  5. Use our calculator if you want personalized macros. It takes 2 minutes and removes guessing.
  6. Refer to the wiki when questions come up. We've tried to anticipate what you'll wonder about.

Bottom Line

The five questions above are real. They matter. And the fact that you're asking them means you're thinking carefully about this. That's good.

The community is here if you want it. We've got you.
