Our Mission

Carnivore Weekly exists because the carnivore community deserves better curation.

Why We Started This

The carnivore space has grown rapidly. What once was a small group of people experimenting with an all-meat diet has become a genuine movement with hundreds of thousands asking serious questions about health, metabolism, and what it means to eat in alignment with our biology.

"If you're new to carnivore, you face an overwhelming amount of content. Some of it is solid. Some of it is hype. Some of it contradicts other pieces."

But here's the problem: signal is drowning in noise.

And almost none of it distinguishes between proven physiological adaptation and unsubstantiated claims.

What This Isn't

We're Not Here To...

We're not here to sell you supplements. We're not promoting a particular brand or product. We're not pushing ideology. We're focused on one thing: helping you make better decisions about your health based on evidence and community experience.

Start Where You Are

New to Carnivore?

Welcome. Our Wiki section covers the fundamentals: how to start, what to expect during adaptation, how to structure your meals, and how to track your progress.

If you have specific health concerns (hormones, digestion, performance, sleep), explore our blog posts for in-depth coverage with citations and practical approaches.

Experienced Carnivore?

You probably know carnivore works for you. Our job is to help you understand why it works, how to optimize further, and stay updated on what's emerging in the research and community.

Check out the calculator for personalized macro guidance, or browse the Weekly Watch for curated creator content.

Moving Forward

Every week, we'll be curating the best from the carnivore community, analyzing trends, and publishing deep-dive content on specific topics. We'll be honest about what we know, what we don't know, and where the research is still evolving.

Thank you for being here on day one. We're building this for you.
