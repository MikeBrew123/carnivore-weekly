Beginner's Complete Blueprint: How to Actually Start Carnivore

  • The danger zone is days 3-5: Adaptation kicks in. Electrolytes (especially salt) prevent most "keto flu" symptoms.

If you're thinking "I want to try carnivore," you're not alone. Thirty percent of Reddit's carnivore traffic is people asking: "What should I eat?" and "Will this actually work for me?"

Here's what I've learned watching thousands of people start: most fail in week two, not because carnivore doesn't work, but because they didn't know what to expect. Let's fix that.

"Most people fail in week two, not because carnivore doesn't work, but because they didn't know what to expect."

Week 0: Before You Start

Answer This First: Why Are You Doing This?

Not the Instagram answer. The real one. Are you fixing blood sugar crashes? Trying to lose weight? Dealing with digestive issues? Clearing brain fog?

The answer matters because it changes what success looks like:

  • Fat loss: Track weight and how clothes fit
  • Energy: Notice afternoon crashes disappearing
  • Inflammation: Watch joint pain improve
  • Digestion: Monitor bloating and regularity

Also: Talk to your doctor. If you're on medication‚Äîespecially for blood sugar or blood pressure‚Äîyou need medical oversight. Carnivore changes how your body processes medication.

Week 1: The Simple Start

Your job this week is to prove you can execute the diet. That's it. Not to be perfect. Not to hit perfect macros. Just to eat meat.

Week One Meal Plan

Breakfast 6-8 oz ground beef with salt and water
Lunch 8-10 oz steak with salt and water
Dinner 10-12 oz beef roast or ground beef with salt and water
Snacks If hungry: more beef, hard-boiled eggs, or canned sardines

Why beef and salt? Beef is the most nutrient-dense food available. Salt prevents electrolyte crashes that cause headaches and fatigue. Water keeps everything moving. No complexity. No variables.

How much to eat: Until you're satisfied, not stuffed. Your hunger signals will be weird for a few days. That's normal. Listen to satiety, not quantity.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Danger Zone: Days 3-5

This is when adaptation kicks in. You might feel foggy, tired, or get a headache. This is adaptation, not failure.

  • Days 1-2: Probably fine. Some people feel great immediately.
  • Days 3-5: The danger zone. Foggy, tired, headache possible.
  • Days 6-7: Energy usually returns. Sleep might be weird.

If you get a headache: Add salt (1/4 teaspoon with water) and eat more. Most "keto flu" is dehydration + electrolyte depletion, not the diet failing.

Weeks 2-4: The Adaptation Phase

Your Body Is Learning

This is where your digestive system learns that you're only eating meat. Your gut bacteria shift. Your stomach acid production normalizes. Your liver adjusts to processing fat at scale.

This takes time. Not magic time. Physiological time.

What changes now: You can start experimenting. Try different beef sources. Add eggs. Try pork, lamb, or fish. See what feels good. Most people find their preference‚Äîall beef, beef + eggs, or beef + fish. All valid.

Expect:

  • Energy fluctuations‚Äîsome days amazing, some tired
  • Appetite changes‚Äîyour body is figuring out fuel needs
  • Weird sleep for 3-4 weeks (4 hours or 10 hours‚Äîboth normal)

The Electrolyte Reality

This Is Where People Fail

When you stop eating carbs, your body stops storing sodium (carbs bind water and sodium). You'll pee out water and electrolytes for 2-3 weeks. This is why you feel tired or get headaches.

The fix: Salt your food. A lot. More than you think. Aim for 5-10 grams of sodium per day (1-2 teaspoons of salt) for the first 3-4 weeks.

Yes, that sounds like a lot. You're not eating carbs, so you're not spiking insulin or retaining excess water. You need the sodium.

Signs you're low on electrolytes:

Headaches
Fatigue
Muscle cramps
Dizziness

Quick fix: Salt water (1/4 teaspoon in 8oz water) + food. Usually improves within 30 minutes.

Tracking Progress (The Right Way)

Don't just weigh yourself. Weight fluctuates 3-5 lbs daily based on water, hormones, digestion, and sodium. One number tells you almost nothing.

Clothes Fit

More accurate than scale. Track waist, chest, arms.

Energy Levels

Rate 1-10 throughout the day. Notice patterns.

Hunger Patterns

Fasting easier? Less snacking? Natural satiety?

Digestion

How are you feeling? Bloating? Regularity?

Sleep Quality

Not duration‚Äîquality. Waking rested?

Mental Clarity

Is brain fog gone? Focus improved?

Take photos on day 1, day 30, and day 90. You'll see changes the scale won't show.

Common Questions

Q: Will my cholesterol get messed up?

It might change. Some people's LDL goes up, some goes down. Total cholesterol often goes down. HDL almost always goes up. The important metrics are inflammation markers (triglycerides, apoB, hs-CRP), not total cholesterol. Get blood work at 30 and 90 days.

Q: How much do I actually need to eat?

Until satisfied. That's it. Hunger regulation usually improves after week 2. Most people eat less naturally without trying.

Q: Is it okay to do this with exercise?

Yes, but expect to feel flat for 2-3 weeks while adapting. Your glycogen stores are depleted. After that, many people feel stronger. Some do lighter training during adaptation.

Q: What if I get digestive issues?

Change your approach. Eat smaller meals more frequently. Try ground meat instead of whole cuts. Try different beef sources. Your digestion will adapt, but it takes time.

The 90-Day Checkpoint

By day 90, you'll know if this is working. You'll have:

  • Adapted metabolically (no more adaptation flu)
  • Established your preferred food selections
  • Seen measurable changes (weight, energy, how you look/feel)
  • Built the habit (it's automatic now)
  • Know how your body responds

If you feel dramatically better at day 90, you've found something that works. If not, you have good data about why‚Äîand that's also valuable.

What to Do Right Now

  1. Buy beef (ground or steak, fatty cuts are better)
  2. Buy salt
  3. Plan meals for the next 3 days
  4. Tell your doctor
  5. Take a baseline photo and write down how you feel
  6. Commit to 30 days minimum‚Äînot forever, just 30 days

Key Takeaways

  1. Start simple: Beef, salt, water. Don't overcomplicate week one.
  2. Electrolytes matter: Aim for 5-10g sodium daily during adaptation (weeks 1-4).
  3. The danger zone is days 3-5: If you feel bad, add salt and eat more. Don't quit.
  4. Track what actually matters: Energy, clothes fit, digestion‚Äînot just scale weight.
  5. Commit to 30 days minimum: Real adaptation takes 2-4 weeks. Best results at day 90.

That's it. You're ready.
