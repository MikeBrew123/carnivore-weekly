The First Date: Stealth Mode Activated

Real talk: Don't lead with the diet. Not on your profile, not in the first conversation, not at dinner.

Why? Because "I only eat meat" sounds extreme to people who don't know the context. They haven't read the research. They haven't experienced what it's like to eliminate foods that cause inflammation. All they hear is "this person has weird food rules"‚Äîand that triggers alarm bells.

The move: Let them meet you first. The healthy, confident, interesting version of you. Then, if it comes up later, they've already decided they like you.

If They Ask: The Simple Answer

Sometimes they notice. You're not eating the bread. You ordered your burger without the bun. You skipped the fries.

"Are you doing keto or something?"

Your line:
"I'm experimenting with cutting out foods that were causing inflammation. Turns out I feel way better on mostly meat and fat. Still figuring it out, but it's working so far."

Why this works:

  • It's personal‚Äîabout you, not a crusade
  • It's experimental‚Äîyou're not dogmatic
  • It's results-focused‚Äî"feeling better" is hard to argue with
  • It's vague enough‚Äîdoesn't sound extreme

What NOT to say on a first date:

  • "Vegetables cause disease" (conspiracy theorist vibes)
  • Anything about seed oils, lectins, or oxalates (save it for date five)

You're not lying. You're just not launching into a nutritional lecture while they're trying to enjoy their pasta.

When It Gets Serious: The Conversation

If you're dating someone for a few weeks and it's going well, eventually you'll need to actually talk about it.

Week 2-3

The Natural Mention

They've noticed your eating patterns. Time for a casual acknowledgment without making it a big deal.

Week 4-6

The Real Conversation

If things are progressing, have an actual conversation about it. Be open to their questions.

How to bring it up:
"Hey, I wanted to mention something. I've been eating this way for [X months], and it's made a huge difference in how I feel. I know it probably seems weird from the outside, but it's working for me. I'm not trying to convince you to do it‚ÄîI just wanted you to understand why I eat the way I do."

Then let them ask questions. Most people are curious, not hostile, once they see that you're healthy and reasonable.

The Bottom Line

Dating while carnivore doesn't have to be complicated. You're not hiding who you are. You're just not making your diet the first thing people learn about you.

Pick restaurants where your food choices blend in. Keep your explanations simple and non-preachy. And if someone can't handle that you eat differently than they do, that's useful information about compatibility.

You're looking for someone who respects your choices, even if they don't make the same ones. And honestly? That's a good filter for a healthy relationship anyway.
