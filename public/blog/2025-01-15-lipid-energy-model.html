The Lipid Energy Model: Understanding Lean Mass Hyper-Responders

Some people on carnivore get shredded. Lean. Muscular. No effort, just eating meat.

Others struggle. They don't lose fat as easily. They feel tired. Something's off.

The difference is often the lipid energy model and how well you're adapted to burning fat.

The Model

Your body can run on two fuels: glucose and lipids (fat). Most people default to glucose. Their bodies are "glucose dependent."

On carnivore, you're forcing an adaptation to lipids. Some bodies adapt easily (hyper-responders). Some take longer.

Hyper-responders feel amazing immediately. Energy up. Fat dropping. Performance improving.

Non-responders take longer. They need more electrolytes, more fat, more patience.

How to Know Which You Are

Week one of carnivore: Do you feel amazing or do you feel flat?

If you feel amazing, you're responding well to lipid energy. Lean in. Keep going.

If you feel flat, your body's still trying to adapt. Increase electrolytes and fat. Give it time.
