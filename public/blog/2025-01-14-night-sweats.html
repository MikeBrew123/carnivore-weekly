The Night Sweats: Understanding Temperature Spikes During Adaptation

You start carnivore. First week feels great. Then week two hits and you're waking up at 3 AM absolutely drenched.

Your bed looks like you showered in it. This is weird and uncomfortable.

It's also temporary and actually a sign your metabolism is shifting.

Why This Happens

Your mitochondria are adapting to burn fat instead of carbs. This process generates heat. Your body temperature rises. You sweat to cool down. By 3-4 AM when your body is deepest in rest, you hit the worst of it.

It usually lasts 1-3 weeks. Then it stops.

How to Handle It

Light sheets. Cotton. Keep your bedroom cool. And just know it's temporary. By week four you'll be sleeping through the night like normal, better rested than you've been in years.

The night sweats are your body literally burning through the adaptation process.
