The Exact Electrolyte Protocol: How Much You Need and When (Part 2)

January 25, 2026 by Chloe

In Part 1, we covered why electrolyte deficiency is happening to so many new carnivores right now. Now let's get to the part everyone's been asking for: the exact protocol. How much do you actually need? What supplements work? And how fast will you feel better?

I went deep on this. Talked to experienced carnivores, read the research, and tracked what's actually working in the community. Here's the protocol.

The Three Electrolytes That Matter

Forget the fancy stuff. On carnivore, you need three things:

  • Sodium ‚Äì The big one. Most people are getting way too little.
  • Potassium ‚Äì Important for muscle function and heart rhythm.
  • Magnesium ‚Äì Critical for sleep, cramps, and hundreds of enzymatic processes.

That's it. Calcium, zinc, and other electrolytes? You're getting them from meat. These three are the ones that get depleted when you drop carbs.

The Exact Numbers

Based on research (Phinney & Volek's work on ketogenic athletes) and community experience:

Sodium: 4,000-7,000mg per day

  • Yes, that's way more than the standard "2,300mg limit" you've heard.
  • That limit is for people eating carbs. You're not.
  • Your kidneys excrete sodium faster on low-carb. You need more to maintain balance.
  • Translation: 2-3 teaspoons of salt per day.

Potassium: 3,000-4,700mg per day

  • You get some from meat (especially beef), but probably not enough initially.
  • Signs you're low: muscle weakness, cramps, heart palpitations.
  • Lite Salt or Nu-Salt (potassium chloride) can help bridge the gap.

Magnesium: 300-400mg per day

  • Most people are already deficient before they even start carnivore.
  • Signs you're low: leg cramps at night, trouble sleeping, twitchy muscles.
  • Magnesium glycinate or citrate work best. Avoid oxide (poor absorption).

The Practical Protocol

Week 1-2 (Acute Phase):

  1. Salt everything liberally. If it doesn't taste too salty, add more.
  2. Add 1/4 teaspoon of Lite Salt to 32oz water, drink throughout the day.
  3. Take 200-400mg magnesium glycinate before bed.
  4. If symptoms are severe: drink a glass of bone broth with extra salt added.

Week 3+ (Maintenance):

  1. Keep salting your food generously.
  2. Listen to your body ‚Äì cramps mean more potassium/magnesium, dizziness means more sodium.
  3. Continue magnesium at night if you're prone to cramps or poor sleep.

Timeline: When Will You Feel Better?

Based on community reports:

  • Hours to 1 day: Acute symptoms (dizziness, headache) often resolve within hours of proper supplementation.
  • 2-3 days: Energy levels start normalizing. Brain fog lifts.
  • 1 week: Most people feel fully adapted and symptoms are gone.
  • 2-4 weeks: Your body recalibrates. Many people need less supplementation over time as their body adjusts.

If you're still having issues after 2 weeks of proper supplementation, something else might be going on. Could be eating too little, could be a thyroid issue, could be something else entirely. But for 90% of people, this protocol fixes it.

The Quick-Start Cheat Sheet

If you just want to know what to do RIGHT NOW:

  1. Add 1/2 teaspoon salt to a glass of water. Drink it.
  2. Salt your next meal heavily.
  3. Get some magnesium glycinate and take 200-400mg tonight.
  4. If you have Lite Salt, add 1/4 teaspoon to your water bottle.

You should feel noticeably better within 24-48 hours. If not, increase the sodium and make sure you're eating enough food.

Common Mistakes

"I'm drinking tons of water!" ‚Äì Without electrolytes, you're just flushing more electrolytes out. Water follows sodium. More water without more salt = more depletion.

"I already salt my food." ‚Äì Salt more. Seriously. On carnivore, your old reference point for "enough salt" is probably wrong.

"I don't want to take supplements." ‚Äì Then get your sodium from salt and your potassium from meat (especially beef). Magnesium is harder to get from meat alone, but some people do fine without supplementing if they eat enough.

Bottom Line

Electrolyte deficiency is the #1 reason people quit carnivore in the first month. It's also the easiest thing to fix. Salt your food, add electrolytes to your water, take some magnesium at night. That's it. Within a week, you'll probably feel better than you have in years.
