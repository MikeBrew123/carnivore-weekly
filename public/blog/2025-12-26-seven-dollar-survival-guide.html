The Seven Dollar Survival Guide: Cheapest Carnivore Foods

Performance Coach
Marcus focuses on real-world results and performance optimization.

One of the biggest objections to carnivore is cost. "It's too expensive. I can't afford meat every day." Here's what that usually means: you're buying expensive meat.

Carnivore doesn't have to be expensive. It can be cheaper than most standard diets. Here's how to do it on a budget.

The Cheap Cuts That Work

Ground Beef

$3-5/lb

The MVP of budget carnivore. Fatty ground beef (80/20 or 73/27) is nutrient-dense and versatile. 10-pound chubs at Costco run $3-3.50/lb.

Chuck Roasts

$2-4/lb

Cheap, fatty, forgiving. Throw in a pot, cook slow, eat for days. A 3-pound chuck roast for $8 is a week of meals.

Beef Liver

$2-4/lb

Cheap because people don't want it. More for you. More nutrient-dense than muscle meat. A 1-pound package is often $2-3.

Chicken Thighs

$1.50-3/lb

Cheaper than breasts and have more fat. A rotisserie chicken is $5-7 and provides 3-4 meals.

Canned Fish

$1-3/can

Sardines and mackerel last forever and are nutrient-dense. No prep. No cooking. $2 a meal on sale.

Eggs

$2-4/dozen

Protein and fat. Cheap. A dozen eggs is a week of breakfasts at $0.25-0.35 per egg.

The Buying Strategy

Buy what's on sale. Costco membership is $60/year and pays for itself in meat savings within two months if you buy in bulk.

Shop the sales. Most grocery stores have meat sales every 2-3 weeks. Buy when ground beef hits $3/lb. Stock for three weeks. Skip it when it's $5/lb.

Stock your freezer. When prices are good, buy extra. A chest freezer ($150-200) pays for itself within 6 months of strategic buying.

What Not to Buy

Grass-fed premium ($2-4 more per pound for negligible difference on a budget)
Organic labels (you're only eating the meat, doesn't matter for carnivore)
Expensive cuts (ribeye, sirloin, filet) ‚Äî chuck roast tastes better slow-cooked
Single-serving portions (always more expensive per pound)
Pre-marinated or pre-seasoned meats (paying for salt and marketing)

Prep Strategy on a Budget

Cook in bulk. Sunday: brown 5 pounds of ground beef, season with salt. Portion into containers. Eat from containers all week. Takes 30 minutes, saves you cooking every day.

Or: buy chuck roasts, throw in slow cooker for 8 hours. Meat falls apart. Eat for days. Minimal effort, minimal cost, maximum simplicity.

Where People Overpay

They buy expensive cuts when cheap cuts work just as well. They don't buy in bulk. They don't freeze and plan. They buy single-serving portions. They buy the premium organic grass-fed when regular works fine.

"Budget carnivore isn't about sacrificing quality. It's about not paying premium prices for unnecessary extras."

The Budget-Focused Take

Carnivore can be one of the cheapest diets if you buy the right cuts, buy in bulk, and plan ahead. Ground beef, chuck roasts, chicken, eggs, and canned fish will keep you carnivore for $5-7 per day. That's cheaper than eating out once, and you'll feel better than any processed food diet.

Key Takeaways

  1. Ground beef is MVP: 80/20 at $3-5/lb provides complete nutrition. Buy 10-lb chubs at Costco for best value.
  2. Chuck roasts win slow: $2-4/lb, throw in slow cooker, eat for days. Tastes better than expensive cuts.
  3. Skip the premiums: Grass-fed and organic add cost without proportional benefit on a budget.
  4. Bulk cook Sundays: 30 minutes of cooking = all week's meals prepped. No daily decisions.
  5. $150-240/month total: Cheaper than most diets when you cut the snacks and eating out.
