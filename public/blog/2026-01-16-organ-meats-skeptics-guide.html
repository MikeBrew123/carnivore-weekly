Organ Meats for Skeptics: How to Actually Start Eating Them

TL;DR

  • You don't have to eat organs to do carnivore well. But if you want maximum nutrient density, they're hard to beat.
  • Liver is the easiest starting point. One 3-4 oz serving per week covers most micronutrient bases.
  • The "hide it in ground beef" method works. Mix 1 part liver to 4 parts ground beef. You won't taste it.
  • Whole organs beat supplements for absorption and cost. But capsules work if you truly can't stomach the real thing.

Here's what I hear every week: "Do I HAVE to eat organs on carnivore?"

Short answer: No.

Long answer: Also no. But you're leaving results on the table.

I get it. Liver looks weird. It smells weird. Your grandmother made it terribly and now you have trauma. I was the same way for the first year of carnivore. Steak and eggs only. And it worked fine.

Then I actually tried liver the right way. My energy changed within two weeks. Sleep got deeper. Recovery got faster. The math doesn't lie, and neither did my performance metrics.

So here's the real talk on organs. Not the "you must eat nose-to-tail or you're doing it wrong" gatekeeping. Just the practical stuff that actually matters.

Why Organs Actually Matter (The Numbers)

Stop overthinking it. Organs are just concentrated nutrition.

A ribeye is great. High protein, good fats, tastes amazing. But it's mostly the same nutrients repeated. Muscle meat is muscle meat.

Liver? Different story entirely.

That's not a small difference. That's a completely different food.

One serving of liver per week covers your micronutrient bases better than any multivitamin. And your body actually absorbs it because it comes in the right form, bound to the right co-factors, in a food matrix your digestive system evolved to handle.

One 3-4 oz serving of liver per week. That's it. You don't need to eat it daily. You don't need variety. Just liver, once a week, done.

The Beginner Protocol (Start Here)

Here's the protocol. No complexity, no overthinking.

The 4-Week Organ Introduction

1
Week 1: Ground Beef Blend
Buy 1 lb grass-fed ground beef and 4 oz liver. Grind the liver (or ask your butcher to do it). Mix 1 part liver to 4 parts beef. Cook as burgers or meatballs. Season normally. You will not taste the liver.
2
Week 2: Increase the Ratio
Try 1 part liver to 3 parts beef. Still masked in flavor. If you notice the liver taste, that's fine. Keep going.
3
Week 3: Try Liver Pills (Frozen)
Cut raw liver into pill-sized pieces. Freeze them. Swallow 3-5 frozen pieces with water like supplements. No taste at all. Sounds weird, works great.
4
Week 4: Cook It Properly
Slice liver thin (1/4 inch). Sear in butter or tallow for 90 seconds per side. Don't overcook. Medium-rare is the goal. Salt generously. Eat it hot.

By week 4, you've either developed a taste for liver or you know that the blend method is your long-term strategy. Either works.

The frozen pill method is legitimately good if you never want to taste organs. People do this for years. No shame in it.

Prep Methods That Don't Taste Scary

The number one reason people hate liver: overcooking. Overcooked liver tastes like iron-flavored rubber. Medium-rare liver tastes like rich, mineral-dense meat.

Method 1: The Blend (Beginner)

Easiest
  1. Get liver ground at the butcher (or pulse in food processor)
  2. Mix 1 part liver to 4 parts ground beef
  3. Form into patties or meatballs
  4. Cook as you normally would
  5. Season with salt, maybe some garlic powder
Why it works: The fat and flavor from ground beef masks the liver completely. Kids eat this without knowing.

Method 2: Frozen Pills (Zero Taste)

Easiest
  1. Cut raw liver into small pill-sized pieces
  2. Spread on parchment paper, freeze solid
  3. Store in freezer bag
  4. Swallow 4-6 pieces daily with water
  5. No chewing, no tasting, just nutrition
Pro tip: Do this first thing in the morning on an empty stomach. They go down easier.

Method 3: Pan Seared (The Real Way)

Medium
  1. Slice liver thin, about 1/4 inch thick
  2. Pat completely dry with paper towels
  3. Heat butter or tallow in cast iron until smoking
  4. Sear 60-90 seconds per side (medium-rare center)
  5. Salt generously, eat immediately while hot
The rule: If it's gray all the way through, you overcooked it. Pink center = correct.

Supplements vs. Whole Organs: The Honest Take

I get asked about desiccated organ supplements constantly. Here's my take.

Head to Head

Whole Organs

  • Better absorption (food matrix intact)
  • Cheaper per serving ($0.50-1.00)
  • Complete nutrient profile
  • Fresh if sourced locally
  • You know exactly what you're eating

Desiccated Capsules

  • Zero taste, zero prep
  • Expensive ($1-2 per serving)
  • Some nutrients lost in processing
  • Convenient for travel
  • Works if you truly can't eat organs

My verdict: Try whole organs first. The frozen pill method makes it just as convenient as supplements. But if you've genuinely tried and can't do it, capsules are better than nothing.

What About Other Organs?

Liver is the priority. If you're only going to eat one organ, liver is the right choice.

But if you want to expand:

  • Heart: Tastes like lean steak. High in CoQ10. Easy entry point if liver is too intense.
  • Kidney: Stronger flavor, needs proper cleaning. High in selenium and B12.
  • Tongue: Technically not an organ. Tastes like fatty beef. Very approachable.
  • Brain: Highest in DHA and phospholipids. Creamy texture. Not for beginners.

But honestly? Just do liver. One organ, once a week, done. Don't overcomplicate it.

The Real Talk

You don't need organs to do carnivore successfully. People thrive on muscle meat alone. This isn't gatekeeping, it's just optimization.

If you want the extra edge, better energy, improved recovery, deeper sleep, then organs are the cheapest, most effective upgrade available. One small serving of liver per week. That's the protocol.

Start with the blend method. Graduate to frozen pills if you want convenience. Eventually try it cooked properly. You might be surprised.

I resisted organs for a year. Now I look forward to my weekly liver. Your taste adapts. Your results improve. The math doesn't lie.

Key Takeaways

  1. Organs are optional but powerful. You can do carnivore without them, but you're missing the most nutrient-dense foods available.
  2. Liver is the only one that matters. If you're going to eat one organ, liver covers 90% of the micronutrient benefits.
  3. Start with the blend method. Mix ground liver into ground beef at a 1:4 ratio. You won't taste it.
  4. Frozen liver pills work. Cut raw liver into pill-sized pieces, freeze, swallow with water. Zero taste, full nutrition.
  5. Whole organs beat supplements. Better absorption, cheaper, and the frozen pill method is just as convenient.

Stop overthinking it. Try liver once. See what happens.
