Why Did We Build the Complete Carnivore Protocol?

The Science Problem We Kept Seeing

I've worked with hundreds of people trying carnivore. The pattern was always the same: they'd start strong, feel great for a few weeks, then hit a wall. Fatigue. Stalls. Confusion about what they were doing wrong.

The problem wasn't commitment. It was information.

Generic advice like "eat meat until full" works for some people. But most need actual numbers. How much protein for their body? How much fat for their goal? What does a real day of eating look like?

Research from Dr. Donald Layman and Dr. Stuart Phillips at McMaster University shows protein needs vary dramatically based on lean mass, activity level, and goals. Someone trying to lose fat needs different targets than someone building muscle. And most people, I've found, are undereating protein by 20-30 grams daily while overshooting fat.

That's why I insisted the report include the science. Not just "here are your macros" but "here's WHY these are your macros." Understanding the reasoning helps you adapt when life gets complicated.

The Protocol Gap

Here's what I kept seeing with clients: they had the motivation but no system. They'd ask "what should I eat?" and get vague answers. Eat fatty cuts. Prioritize beef. Salt to taste.

That's not a protocol. That's a suggestion.

A real protocol gives you specifics. For a 180 lb person targeting fat loss, that means: 140-168g protein daily. Around 100g fat. Roughly 1,500-1,800 calories. Hit those numbers and you'll see results. Miss them and you'll wonder why nothing's working.

The Complete Protocol report gives you your exact numbers, plus a meal plan showing exactly how to hit them. No guesswork. No "just eat intuitively and hope for the best."

I've coached athletes who paid hundreds of dollars for less detailed protocols than what we're offering for $9.99. Platforms like MeatRx charge $150+ for coaching packages with less specificity. The math doesn't lie. This is the best value in carnivore nutrition.
