The Deep Freezer Strategy: Half-Cow vs. Weekly Grocery Trips (The Math)

Half cow from a local farm: $800-1200. Yields 150-180 pounds of meat. That's $5-8 per pound all-in.

Grocery store beef: $10-16 per pound for quality cuts.

Over a year, one half-cow saves you $1200-2000 versus grocery shopping.

The Real Win

It's not just cost. It's consistency. You never run out. You always have meat. Your diet never breaks because you're stuck at a grocery store with no beef in stock.

Plus, farm beef is better. Higher omega-3s, more nutrients, and you know the source.

The Setup

Split a half-cow with someone. Get a chest freezer ($300, lasts 10 years). Stock up. Done.

This is the play for long-term carnivore. Not price gouging at Whole Foods every week.
