Frequently Asked Questions

What is the carnivore diet?

The carnivore diet is a meat-based eating approach popularized by Dr. Shawn Baker, author of "The Carnivore Diet," and Dr. Paul Saladino, who wrote "The Carnivore Code." You eat animal products only: beef, pork, chicken, fish, eggs, and some dairy. No vegetables, fruits, or grains. Many carnivore followers eat nose-to-tail, including organ meats like liver for maximum nutrients.

How much protein do I need on carnivore?

Dr. Donald Layman's research shows most adults need 1.0-1.6g protein per pound of lean body mass, depending on your goal. Fat loss? Aim higher (1.2-1.6g). Maintenance? 0.8-1.0g works. Most people undereat protein by 20-30g daily. Apps like Cronometer can help you track if you're hitting your target. Our calculator figures out your personalized number in 90 seconds.

How do I calculate carnivore macros?

The standard approach uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your base metabolism, then adjusts for activity. First, calculate your lean body mass (total weight minus body fat). Set protein based on Dr. Layman's research for your goal. Fat fills the remaining calories. Our free calculator handles this math automatically. If you want to track manually, Cronometer makes it simple.

Do I need to track calories on carnivore?

Not always. The r/carnivore community on Reddit shows many people thrive eating to satiety without tracking. But tracking helps if you've stalled, want muscle gain, or feel exhausted. Apps like Cronometer or MyFitnessPal make it painless. I'd say: try intuitive eating for 30 days first. If results stall, knowing your numbers removes the guesswork.

What's the difference between carnivore and keto?

Keto allows 20-50g carbs from vegetables, nuts, and low-carb foods. Carnivore eliminates all plants. Dr. Ken Berry, a popular keto advocate, often discusses both approaches. Dr. Shawn Baker focuses strictly on carnivore. Both achieve ketosis, but carnivore is simpler: just meat, eggs, and animal products. Carnivore also tends to prioritize higher protein than traditional keto.
