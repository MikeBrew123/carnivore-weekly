How do I know if I'm getting enough fat vs. too much protein?

Hey there! When it comes to balancing fat and protein on a carnivore diet, it's all about understanding your body's unique metabolic signals. Research suggests that the ideal macronutrient ratio can vary based on individual factors like activity level, metabolic health, and personal goals. Using continuous glucose monitors and tracking ketone levels can help you dial in your specific needs. A good starting point is aiming for approximately 70-80% of calories from fat, with protein around 20-30%. Symptoms like persistent hunger, energy crashes, or difficulty maintaining ketosis might indicate you're not hitting the right fat-to-protein balance. Recent metabolic research indicates that protein intake beyond 1.6-2.2 grams per kg of body weight can potentially trigger gluconeogenesis, which might bump you out of the metabolic state you're seeking. My recommendation? Start tracking your biomarkers. Tools like Levels CGM or Oura Ring can give you real-time insights into how different macronutrient ratios impact your metabolism.

Research suggests that while beef contains some thiamine, a carnivore diet might put you at risk for potential deficiencies. Beef isn't the most robust source of vitamin B1, and a strict carnivore approach could lead to marginal intake. Most beef provides around 0.04-0.06 mg of thiamine per 100g, which is significantly lower than recommended daily intake (1.1-1.2 mg for adults). My tracker data from continuous glucose monitoring and blood panels shows nutrient diversity is key - and beef alone might not hit those optimization markers.
