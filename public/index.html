This Week's Roundup

- Chloe, Community Scout

Okay so this week was interesting, the big carnivore YouTube channels were pumping out content but honestly? A lot of it felt like recycled "weight loss secrets" territory. Dr. Berry dropped a Q&A with Neisha tackling "7 Weight Loss Lies" which, fair, we've all heard some doozies from well-meaning relatives. But I'm more intrigued by the smaller creators quietly racking up views, THE STORY channel pulled over 4 million views on carnivore content this week, and iRick Wiggins is steadily climbing too. Feels like the conversation's spreading beyond the usual suspects.

What I'm actually noticing in the community though? People are getting real about the social weirdness of this lifestyle, not just the food science. Like yeah, we know the diet works, but navigating a work lunch or a family dinner without becoming a walking TED talk is its own skill. (Shameless plug but I literally just wrote about that exact thing if you need backup for your next potluck interrogation.)

Here's what I keep wondering though, as carnivore content gets more mainstream and the view counts climb, are we gonna lose some of the nuance? The best stuff I see isn't "lose 50 pounds in 30 days" clickbait, it's people sharing what actually happened when they stuck with it through the weird parts. What's keeping you engaged with carnivore content these days, the big splashy stuff or the quieter real-talk posts?

This Is The New Butter (And You’ll Be FORCED To Eat It!)

Okay, so the community's rage here is real, and honestly the butter thing hits different because it's one of our staples. People are waking up to the fact that what's in the package keeps changing while the label stays basically the same, and that's the vibe that's resonating hardest. The local farm comment is huge too, because on carnivore we're already thinking about food quality in ways most people aren't, so sourcing directly from farmers you actually know might be the move sooner than we thought. This isn't just about butter anymore, it's about whether we can actually trust what we're buying, and the community is saying loud and clear: probably not.

I'm Eating This ENTIRE Pizza Everyday and Losing Weight

One thing I've noticed from coaching is that people don't quit carnivore because the food isn't healthy, they quit because they miss the *ritual* of eating pizza on a Friday night. This recipe solves that problem in a way that actually works. The cottage cheese crust is genius because it gives you that savory, satisfying texture without the blood sugar spike that derails so many people trying to stick with this. What's wild is watching the comments, people are reporting they're losing weight *while* eating this regularly, which tells you everything you need to know about how different carnivore comfort foods are from their processed versions. If pizza's been your sticking point, this changes the game.

🔥 Science KenDBerryMD

7 Weight Loss Lies + Q&A with Dr Berry & Nurse Neisha

Real talk: the comment section is full of people who spent decades believing "fat makes fat" and are now shocked they're leaner than ever eating butter and bacon. That's not just weight loss, that's deprogramming. What gets me most is the Appalachian nurse who had to restart after her first attempt crashed hard because of undiagnosed celiac and hidden gluten in processed meats. She's the reason we talk about paying attention to quality and understanding your own body before jumping in, not just counting carbs and hoping for the best. The wins here span everyone from the 68-year-old dude who lost 170 lbs total (keto plus carnivore) to the woman doing OMAD at 57 maintaining muscle and a 4.5 A1C. These aren't outliers anymore, they're patterns. When that many people break the same old rule and thrive, maybe the rule was wrong all along.
