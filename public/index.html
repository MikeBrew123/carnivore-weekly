Common Questions Answered

How long before I see blood pressure improvements?

Most people report 20-point drops within 3 months. Some see changes within weeks. The mechanism: reduced inflammation, improved metabolic efficiency, and better nutrient absorption affect cardiovascular function directly.

Will my LDL cholesterol spike? Is that bad?

LDL may increase initially. Important context: LDL particle size matters more than the number. Cardiologists in the community confirm that large, fluffy LDL particles (which you'll have on carnivore) are much less atherogenic than small, dense particles. Get a full lipid panel, not just LDL number.

How much salt do I actually need?

More than you think. Calculate: (activity level in hours √ó 1.5 liters) + (climate heat factor). Most athletes are under-salting on carnivore and experiencing fatigue unnecessarily. Cardiologist-validated: salt intake on carnivore should be 5-7g daily for active people.

Is carnivore sustainable long-term for women?

Absolutely. Women report stable cycles, improved energy, and hormonal balance. The key: adequate calories and micronutrient density (organ meats weekly). Hormone stability often improves because stable fat-based energy prevents the cortisol spikes that come from carb fluctuations.

How do I know if my IBS will resolve?

IBS is almost always triggered by something in plant foods. Grass-fed carnivore eliminates all plant antigens. Timeline: 50% improvement by week 2, 80% by week 4, often complete resolution by week 8-12.
