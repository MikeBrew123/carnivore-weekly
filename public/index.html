What does the evidence say about carnivore and cholesterol?

Strong Evidence

Cholesterol on carnivore works differently than on other diets. LDL typically increases, but particle size improvements and metabolic markers often show significant benefits. Most n=1 experiments show improved triglycerides and HDL/LDL ratios despite higher total cholesterol.

Source: Peer-reviewed studies, n=1000+ case reports

How long until the adaptation phase ends?

Moderate Evidence

Most people report 2-4 weeks of transition symptoms (fatigue, headaches, electrolyte imbalances). Full metabolic adaptation takes 8-12 weeks. Individual variation is high based on carb history and activity level.

Source: Community data, circadian biology research

Why do some people experience amenorrhea on carnivore?

Emerging Evidence

Multiple mechanisms: rapid weight loss, electrolyte imbalances, very low carb intake affecting reproductive hormones, or unmasking existing hormone imbalances. Most cases resolve with carb cycling or increasing electrolytes. Some women report increased fertility and cycle regularity.

Source: Endocrinology literature, clinician observations
