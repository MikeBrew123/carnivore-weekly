This Week's Roundup

- Chloe, Community Scout

Okay, so this week the carnivore community is absolutely obsessed with comfort food hacks, and honestly? I'm here for it. Steak and Butter Gal dropped a video claiming her carnivore recipe "destroyed" her carb cravings, and with over 325K views, clearly people are hungry for solutions that don't involve white-knuckling through bread withdrawals. She's talking $5 ingredients, cream cheese, gelatin, and raw dairy, basically building carnivore pound cake from scratch. The comments are probably already flooded with "OMG I needed this" and the inevitable "but is allulose really carnivore?" debates.

Here's what I'm noticing though: the community keeps circling back to this tension between "strict meat and water" purists and the folks who want their carnivore cheesecake too. And you know what? Both camps are thriving. The fact that comfort food recipes are pulling these kinds of numbers tells me people are settling into this way of eating for the long haul, they're not just doing a 30-day challenge, they're building a lifestyle. When you start perfecting your zero-carb pound cake game, that's commitment.

What strikes me most this week is how the conversation has shifted from "can I survive without carbs?" to "how do I make this sustainable and actually enjoyable?" That's maturity, folks. We've moved past the survival phase and into the "I'm hosting a dinner party and yes, everything on this table came from an animal" phase. The carnivore community isn't just eating differently, we're getting creative about it.
